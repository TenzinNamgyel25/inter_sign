Do you know about Sign Language?

Sign language is a rich and expressive form of communication that uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body language to convey meaning. It is a vital tool for the hard-of-hearing communities, providing access to education, work, and social interaction. Different regions have their own sign languages, such as American Sign Language (ASL) and British Sign Language (BSL), each with unique grammar and structure. Beyond accessibility, sign language fosters inclusivity and bridges communication gaps, making the world more connected for everyone.

American Sign Language

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language used by the hearing impaired community in the United States and parts of Canada. It has its own unique grammar, syntax, and structure, distinct from English, relying on hand movements, facial expressions, and body language to convey meaning. ASL is not simply a visual representation of English but a fully developed language with regional dialects and variations. It plays a crucial role in Deaf culture, fostering communication, identity, and community among its users. Over the years, ASL has gained recognition as an official language, and efforts to promote its accessibility in education, media, and public spaces continue to grow.

What is the process?

The three steps from here to your first interpretation

1. Get Set Up

Open Intersign and give it the green light (aka permissions) for the camera and mic so it can "see" your signs!

2. Strike a Pose

Make sure you're in a well-lit area with a clear background—no sneaky distractions behind you! Position yourself so the camera gets a full view of your hands and face.

3. Start Signing

Sign at a steady, natural pace—no need to rush! If something looks off, try signing again or tweak your hand positioning for better accuracy.
